About

one idea, evolving

It started as curiosity about how machines could understand the world. That curiosity became code, the code reached into hardware, and the hardware learned to perceive.

Today I'm an engineering undergraduate focused on computer vision and robotics — building real systems that combine perception, control, and software engineering: autonomous underwater vehicles, vision-driven robotic arms, and applied machine-learning pipelines, through robotics teams, internships, and technical leadership roles.

The problems I chase sit at the seam between hardware and intelligent software — and the same instinct extends to people: leading teams, mentoring engineers, and building technical communities where systems thinking spreads.
